Onboarding Queue & Initial TV Visit

Select the visit classification used for onboarding and documentation.
Use Modality to distinguish in-person vs remote visits; Visit Type captures purpose.
Enter the completion date of the visit, not the documentation date.
Include assessment findings, interventions, patient response, and plan.
Notes — What to write:
  • Reason for visit and context (e.g., intake, care plan review).
  • Key findings: code/cognitive/functional, mental health concerns, major issues.
  • Interventions/orders and education/counseling provided.
  • Risk rationale for chosen Subjective Risk Level.
  • Follow-ups, referrals, and next steps.
Example: "Initial TV (Telehealth). Confirmed DNR; cognitive intact; independent ADLs. Reviewed meds; counseled on fall risks. Risk: Moderate due to 1 ER visit in 12 mo. Plan: home visit in 2 weeks; refer to PT; coordinator to schedule."

Field Explanations
  • Visit Type: Clinical purpose (Initial TV, Intake Follow-up, Care Plan Review).
  • Modality: In-person Home Visit vs Telehealth; separate from Visit Type.
  • Date: When the visit occurred; drives recency/overdue logic.
  • Visit Notes: Clinical narrative: assessment, response, and plan.
  • Mental Health: Multi-select relevant conditions impacting care.
  • Code Status: Resuscitation preference documented in chart.
  • Cognitive/Functional: Baseline profiles guiding communication and services.
  • GOC Status: New/Revise/Confirm/Established/Needs Review vocabulary.
  • Subjective Risk Level: Low/Moderate/High/Critical based on provider impression.
  • Goals of Care: Patient-centered goals for the care plan.
  • Active Concerns: Prioritized issues to address.
  • Active Specialists: Current specialist involvement and contacts.
  • ER/Hospitalizations (12 mo): Count and optional details; informs risk.
  • Schedule Visit: In live app, saves and removes from onboarding queue when Initial TV complete.

Phone Review Notes — What to write:
  • Call purpose and context.
  • Symptoms/updates, medication adherence/issues.
  • Actions taken (advice, orders, coordination initiated).
  • Next steps (appointments, referrals, monitoring plan).
Example: "Follow-up call re: BP and dizziness. Adherent to meds; reports intermittent dizziness when standing. Advised orthostatic precautions; coordinator to schedule PCP tele-visit. Plan: monitor BP daily; call if worsening."
Required fields are validated in the live form. Saved to provider_tasks for reporting.
